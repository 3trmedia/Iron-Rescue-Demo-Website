Wilderness Medicine

When You're Miles From Help, Training Is Everything

Don't wait for help that's an hour away. Be the help.

When your group is bleeding out at 8,000 feet and the nearest hospital is hours away, theory won't save them. Iron Rescue's Wilderness Medicine course gives everyday people the hands-on skills to stabilize injuries, manage emergencies, and keep someone alive until rescue arrives.

Train With Us See Course Dates

What You'll Master

Bleeding Control
Learn to apply tourniquets, pack wounds, and use hemostatic gauze under pressure. These are the skills that buy someone the time they need to survive.
Airway Management
Know how to keep someone breathing when EMS is hours away. A clear airway is the difference between life and death in remote environments.
Fracture Stabilization
Build improvised splints from available materials and immobilize injuries for transport. You won't always have the right gear — we train you to work with what's there.
Patient Assessment
Run a systematic head-to-toe evaluation to identify life threats and prioritize care. Stay calm, stay methodical, stay effective.
Casualty Movement
Learn to safely move an injured person across backcountry terrain without making injuries worse. Getting someone to safety is as important as treating them on the ground.
Wilderness Decision Making
Train for the moment when resources are limited, help is far, and every decision counts. We put you in the scenario so the real thing never catches you off guard.

Course Structure

A Straightforward Process That Turns Backcountry Enthusiasts Into Confident First Responders.

1
Learn The Material
We start with the medical knowledge that matters most in wilderness emergencies. You'll understand how trauma affects the body, why the first few minutes are critical, and how to make clear-headed decisions when the people around you are counting on you. No jargon. No overwhelm. Just what matters.
2
Practice The Skills
Knowledge without repetition means nothing. You'll rotate through realistic training scenarios — controlling bleeding on simulators, stabilizing fractures with improvised materials, making quick decisions under pressure with instructors coaching you in real time. By the end these skills aren't just learned. They're yours.
3
Acquire The Gear
Every Tier 2 student leaves with a complete Reecoil Deploy Trauma Kit configured specifically for wilderness and backcountry emergencies. Easton walks you through every item so you leave knowing exactly how to use it when it counts.
